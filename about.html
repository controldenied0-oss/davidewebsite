About The Instructor

Learn more about Davide Marini’s background as a performer, researcher, and drum educator.

Davide Marini is a London-based professional drummer and educator. Originally from Cagliari, Italy, his career as a performer has taken him across Europe, the UK, India, Bangladesh, and the United States. A graduate of the University of West London (BMus Popular Music Performance, 2012), his playing combines in-depth pop session training with a lifelong passion for extreme metal and experimental forms of jazz and improvised music.

During his postgraduate studies at the Royal College of Music (MSc Performance Science, 2019), Davide conducted research into professional drummers’ understanding of body mechanics, ergonomics, and movement. His insights into kinanesthesia and efficient physical use at the drum kit now inform both his performance work and his teaching approach.

Today Davide divides his time between teaching, session work, and live performances with his bands Concrete Age and Evisarize. He teaches from his studio in Acton and offers lessons throughout West, North West, and South West London.

Meet Davide

★

Professional drum tuition with a personal approach

Davide has over 12 years of teaching experience and has taught students ranging from age 4 to over 60 years old. Over the years he has guided students through Trinity Drum Grades 1–6, GCSE and A-level music exams, music degree auditions, and other performance assessments.

As a performer he has worked extensively in the recording studio and on stages throughout the UK, Europe, India, and the United States. This professional experience feeds directly into his teaching, helping students develop not just technique, but real musical awareness, confidence, and practical performance skills.

With all of his students Davide aims to set a strong example by being organised, reliable, and supportive, while encouraging them to develop their own musical tastes and consistent practice habits. The goal is always to help students become confident, capable musicians while enjoying the process of learning the instrument.

  • Lessons for students aged 4 to 60+
  • Preparation for Trinity grades, GCSE, A-Level and auditions
  • Teaching informed by performance science and real-world touring experience
